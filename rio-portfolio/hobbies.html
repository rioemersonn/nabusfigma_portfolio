Hobbies

Extreme Ironing

Taking an ironing board to remote locations (like a mountain top or underwater) to iron a shirt. Yes, it's a real competitive sport.

Taxidermy (Anthropomorphic)

Stuffing small animals and dressing them up in tiny human clothes to recreate Victorian tea parties.

Geocaching

Using GPS coordinates to find "treasure" hidden by strangers in the real world. It's basically high-tech hide-and-seek.

Lockpicking

The "puzzle-solving" art of opening locks without a key (for legal, recreational purposes, obviously).

Historical Reenactment

Spending your weekends dressed in 40 lbs of chainmail, pretending it's the year 1250.

Amateur Mycology

Hunting for wild mushrooms in the woods—part science, part "will this make me see colors or kill me?"

Parkour / Free Running

Treating the urban environment like an obstacle course by jumping off walls and over railings.

Underwater Hockey

You wear fins, a snorkel, and try to push a puck across the bottom of a pool.

Larping

Running through a park swinging foam swords and shouting "Lightning Bolt!" at your friends.

Competitive Dog Grooming

Turning a Poodle into a sculpture of a tropical parrot or a pirate ship using safe dyes and shears.
